History and Heritage


Originally, Boksburg was laid out in 1887 to serve the surrounding gold mines, and named after the State Secretary of the South African Republic, Eduard Bok. The Main Reef Road linked Boksburg to all the other major mining towns on the Witwatersrand and the Angelo Hotel was used as a production post.

Population


Boksburg is a large place in the region of Gauteng in South Africa with a population of approximately 445,168 people and is one of the largest places in South Africa.

Attractions and Cultural Sites


Discover Boksburg's captivating attractions by exploring our Theme Park (Gold Reef City), the City of Gold Mines is the largest theme amusement park and leisure entertainment center in South Africa. The city of gold mines is themed around the Witwatersrand Gold Rush. The employees in the theme park are dressed in the costumes of gold diggers from the 1880s, and the design of the park is also imitating the style of that era. There are many free-range peacocks strolling leisurely in the park, passing by from time to time. The theme park is surrounded by hotels, entertainment venues, restaurants, children's museums, 4D cinemas, and a journey into a gold mine. The mines of the Golden City were dug to a depth of 3,200 meters underground, and now tourists can go down to 220 meters underground to see the actual operation of gold mining at that time. Also see the actual melting of gold and the process of casting gold coins.

Apartheid Museum


TThe Apartheid Museum is a museum illustrating apartheid and the 20th-century history of South Africa. The museum, part of the Gold Reef City complex in Johannesburg, was opened in November 2001. It covers South Africa's struggle to freedom and journey to democracy.

Local Cuisine and Culinary Delights


This bustling city is home to some of Gauteng's most exciting and diverse dining destinations, with something to suit every taste and budget. Boksburg has everything from Italian-inspired cuisine to mouthwatering ribs, fragrant curries, and expertly grilled steaks.
